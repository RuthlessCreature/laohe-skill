# GTM、市场与变现参考

Use this reference for ICP, competitor analysis, positioning, pricing, sales, launch, partnerships, and revenue plans.

## Research Discipline

- Verify current facts with live search: market claims, competitors, pricing pages, API limits, regulation, funding, usage numbers, or current events.
- Use exact dates for time-sensitive claims.
- Separate facts, inference, and recommendation.
- Prefer source-backed tables over narrative when comparing competitors or pricing.

## ICP Frame

Define:

- Buyer: budget holder and urgency driver.
- User: daily workflow owner.
- Trigger: event that creates immediate demand.
- Pain: measurable cost of current state.
- Alternative: how they solve it today.
- WTP: willingness to pay, budget source, and approval path.
- Channel: where this person can be reached repeatedly.

## Positioning Formula

Use:

```text
For [ICP] who struggle with [urgent pain],
[product] is a [category] that delivers [quantified outcome]
unlike [alternative], because [credible mechanism/proof].
```

## Pricing Checks

Model at least three packages:

- Entry: low-friction adoption, narrow promise.
- Core: main value capture, best expected fit.
- Premium: high-touch or high-risk customer needs.

Check:

```latex
\text{Payback Months} = \frac{\text{CAC}}{\text{Monthly Gross Profit}}
```

```latex
\text{LTV} = \frac{\text{ARPA} \times \text{Gross Margin}}{\text{Monthly Churn}}
```

```latex
\text{ROI}_{customer} = \frac{\text{Benefit} - \text{Cost}}{\text{Cost}}
```

## Launch Plan

Include:

- Audience segment and offer.
- Message hierarchy: outcome, proof, objection handling.
- Channel mix: founder-led sales, content, outbound, partnerships, marketplaces, community, paid tests.
- Funnel assumptions: impressions, clicks, demos, trials, wins, revenue.
- Launch assets: landing page, demo, case study, pricing page, sales deck, FAQ, onboarding email.
- Learning cadence: daily launch standup, weekly funnel review, monthly pricing review.

## Sales Motion

For B2B or high-consideration products, create:

- Target account list.
- Discovery script.
- Demo storyline.
- ROI calculator.
- Proposal template.
- Objection bank.
- Follow-up sequence.
- Close plan.

## Commercial Red Flags

- User loves it but buyer has no budget.
- Channel is vague or too expensive.
- Pricing captures effort rather than customer value.
- Product saves time but not enough money, risk, or status.
- MVP has no distribution path.
- Metrics stop at usage and never connect to revenue.
